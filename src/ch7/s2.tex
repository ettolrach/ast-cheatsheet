\wde{7.2.2 $\ddownarrow$} Let $\leq$ be an order relation on a set $X$. For $x \in X$, we write $\ddownarrow x$ for the subset $\{ x \in X \colon y < x \}$ of $X$.
\wt{7.2.4 Principle of strong induction} Let $A$ be a subset of $\N$ such that for all $n \in \N$, $(i \in A \text{ for all natural numbers } i < n) \implies n \in AA$. Then $A = \N$.
\wpr{7.2.6} Let $X$ be a set and $(r_n)_{n \in \N} \in \prod_{n \in \N} X^{(X^{\mathbf{n}})}$. Then $\exists!$ function $f \colon \N \to X$ such that $f(n) = r_n (f \mid_{\ddownarrow_n})$ for all $n \in \N$.
